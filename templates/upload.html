Leisler’s bat

Leisler’s bat (Nyctalus leisleri) is a medium-sized bat known for its fast, high flight and shaggy reddish-brown fur.

Common across Ireland, it typically roosts in tree holes but can also be found in buildings.

Leisler’s bats emerge early in the evening to hunt insects in open spaces and are often detected by their loud echolocation calls around 20–25 kHz.

Common pipistrelle

The common pipistrelle (Pipistrellus pipistrellus) is one of the smallest and most widespread bats in Europe.

With a wingspan of around 20–25 cm, it roosts in buildings, trees, and bat boxes, often close to human settlements.

It emerges at dusk to hunt tiny insects using rapid, high-pitched echolocation calls peaking around 45 kHz.

Myotis

Daubenton's bat and Natterer’s bat

Myotis bats (Myotis spp.) are a diverse group of "mouse-eared" bats found across Europe and beyond.

They typically have soft, brownish-grey fur and large, broad ears.

Roosting in trees, caves, and buildings, Myotis species hunt insects over water, woodlands, and meadows, using quiet, high-frequency echolocation.

Common species include Daubenton’s bat and Natterer’s bat, which are identifiable by this model.
